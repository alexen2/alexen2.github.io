Outsourcing vs In-House product design: Which is Better?

Fintech is revolutionizing the app design landscape. The industry’s interfaces must create entirely new security standards, market invented needs to new adopters, and reduce complex concepts to the simplest of terms. That turns traditional arguments for outsourcing back to front like an old sweater. An in-house team who can create an onboarding app can’t necessarily manage the complexity of the stock investment design process. your staff might be competent enough to design a gamified loyalty app, but they may not manage the rigors of full-service banking. Still, there is no single perfect response to the outsourcing/insourcing debate, only layers of complexity.

The Pros and Cons of In-House Development

App design is gruelling work, and it requires people who are eager to grow with you until they become your perfect cultural fit. In-house design is deceptively affordable when you’re combing through your finances prior to the first iteration of your interface. Fast forward a few years, though, and the true costs come into focus. The long term expenses of an in-house app are reflected in:

  • Knowledge and skill gaps, which may require you to scrap and recreate your interface several times before release.
  • The monthly salaries of full-time design teams, which are significantly more expensive over time than the scalable costs of outsourcing.
  • A sloppy design process, which could negate any hope of profits.

If your in-house project’s scope grows, you’ll also need to source new talent, but insourcing isn’t without its benefits. It builds on a foundation of loyalty, letting you train your own team members to expert level. Employees have a working knowledge of your corporate culture and give you full control of your project.

The Benefits can Outweigh the Challenges of Outsourcing

A subpar firm may not be invested enough in your interests to give you a controlling voice in the design process. This is a highly collaborative process that requires a transparent, passionate firm. Choose a company with dwindling profits, and you run the risk of losing the support of the team who designed your app. However, if you’re selective about your choice of contractor and do your due diligence in ensuring they have a solid future, you gain unparalleled expertise. Specialists gain extra knowledge by focusing exclusively on architecture, UX and UI design day after day. They will thus:

  • Evolve with progress in the app design space, allowing them to adopt new technologies and trends early.
  • Have a complete team, including mobile engineers, graphic designers, and UI specialists.
  • Be flexible enough to let you use a mix-sourced design model.
  • Have done work in the fintech niche before, which will have exposed them to innovative marketing tools such as share/reward models, goal-driven personalization, and tools that humanize your app’s UX.

Mixed Models

It’s not just fintech apps that are becoming increasingly personalized. Outsourced teams are just as adept at offering a personalized delivery model. Project-based outsourcing lets you leverage your in-house team for projects that suit their talents, leaving you to outsource the rest. You can hire external designers to craft your initial interface, then maintain it in-house. This way, new companies can gain the upper hand without committing to one service delivery model. You can even use an outsourced team to support your existing hires or pay a retainer to a contractor to advise your employees.

The design process of a product that hasn’t yet hit the market requires long term commitment and a refined understanding of your culture. In-sourcing meets those challenges head-on. However, if your product is an app Goliath rather than a David, the level of expertise you need will probably require you to reach beyond your own stable. Outsourcing will prepare you for post-market conditions and give you information on the competitive landscape that your in-house team isn’t exposed enough to know.

Think of your outsourced designer as the cartographer of your app development «map.» It can zoom all the way out to chart entire continents. In-house teams tend to be too close to land to offer as much perspective. Shared vision is as much a problem as it is an asset. Sometimes, you need a new vision to add nuance to your own, and that’s a role that outsourcing excels at.
